Ongoing killing in Palestine

The conflict between Israel and Palestinians — rooted in decades of tensions over land, governance, and rights — escalated sharply on 7 October 2023 after Hamas attacks and subsequent Israeli military operations. This has led to intense and prolonged fighting, especially in the Gaza Strip and parts of the West Bank.

    Violence and Killings of Palestinians

    • Large numbers of civilian casualties in Gaza
      • Israeli military operations in and around Gaza have continued intermittently, with air strikes and artillery fire killing dozens to hundreds of Palestinians at a time in recent months.
      • These attacks have often occurred despite ceasefire agreements, with reports of violations leading to renewed violence.
    • Recent reported deaths
      • According to recent reports, Israeli strikes and shelling have killed dozens of Palestinians, including children, in multiple incidents across Gaza.
    • Ongoing risks and rights concerns
      • Human rights organizations and the United Nations have expressed concern about ongoing violations against civilians, including killings, restricted humanitarian aid, and damage to civilian infrastructure.

      • Specific documented incidents

      • Rafah paramedic massacre (March 2025): At least 15 Palestinian medics were killed when humanitarian vehicles were struck in southern Rafah, Gaza.
      • “Flour Massacre” (Feb 2024): Over 118 civilians were killed and hundreds wounded while lining up for food aid in Gaza City.
      • Killings in the West Bank: Individual Palestinians — including youth like Mohammad Khdour and Tawfic Abdel Jabbar — were shot and killed in separate incidents of violence in the West Bank.
